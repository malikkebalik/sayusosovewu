    Bryant Young, the former NFL athlete, delivered a heartfelt tribute to his late son, Colby Young, following his induction in the Hall of Fame.

    During the NFL Honors on Thursday night, the Pro Football Hall of Fame announced its class of 2022, with former 49ers defensive tackle Bryant Young rightfully and deservingly joining the ranks of NFL legends.

    Bryant retired from the sport in 2007 after a decorated career with the 49ers. He also has 774 tackles and six fumble recoveries under his belt. 

    Bryant was nominated for the Pro Football Hall of Fame for the second time and was a finalist for the Class of 2020.

    HOF Bryant Young Son: Colby Young Died From Cancer Age 15

    Colby Young, the son of HOF Bryant Young, died at 15 years of age after a long and courageous battle with cancer on October 11, 2016.

    Bryant quizzed his defensive linemen with a total of 15 questions relating to that week's opponent before every game. Although the participants were unaware, he had chosen the number as a tribute to his late son, Colby.

    What To Know About Bryant Young Son?

    Colby Young, the son of Bryant Young, was an eighth-grader in Charlotte, North Carolina.

    Colby Young Illness Explained

    Colby Young suffered from the illness of pediatric brain cancer, the growth of abnormal cells inside the brain.

    In January 2015, Colby began chemotherapy at Duke University, and it took four months to complete.

    When he relapsed, the Youngs found a clinical trial at the University of Florida Shands Hospital that involved immunotherapy, a method in which the body's immune system is used to combat cancer.
