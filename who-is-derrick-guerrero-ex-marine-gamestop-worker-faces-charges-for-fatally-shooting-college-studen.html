    FORT LAUDERDALE, FLORIDA: Derrick Guerrero, a retired Marine and a Florida GameStop employee, has been charged with manslaughter after police claimed he shot and killed a shoplifter who attempted to take $600 worth of Pokemon trading cards.

    The report released by Pembroke Pines police on Wednesday, October 18, stated that 33-year-old Guerrero was working at the video game store in a suburban Fort Lauderdale strip mall on Tuesday night when a man grabbed 5 boxes of Pokemon Scarlet & Violet "ultra-premium" trading cards, each costing $120, and ran toward the door.

    What happened between Derrick Guerrero and Chadrick Coats?

    Guerrero saw a man shoplifting while he was working at the gaming store located at 11069 Pines Blvd, said Pembroke Pines police.

    The suspected shoplifter, Coats, fled for the front door with $600 worth of Pokemon Scarlet & Violet Ultra-Premium Collection trading cards after he was confronted by Guerrero and a struggle broke out.

    According to the police, once the victim dropped the goods and started to flee, Guerrero took a hidden gun out of his belt and shot Coats in the upper torso. He was taken immediately to a nearby hospital where he eventually died.

    The alleged shoplifter never displayed a gun throughout the incident and did not possess a firearm, police said.

    According to investigators, surveillance camera footage shows Guerrero reaching for his revolver and pursuing Coats.
