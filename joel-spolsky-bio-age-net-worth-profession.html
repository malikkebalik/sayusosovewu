    How did Joel start his Professional Career?

    Joel Spolsky started working for Microsoft as a program manager in the Microsoft Excel division in 1991. Both Excel Basic and Microsoft’s Visual Basic for Applications techniques were developed by him. Joel relocated to New York City in 1995 to work for Viacom and Juno Online Services.

    After five years, Joel formed Fog Creek Software and started one of the first company owner blogs with Joel on Software. Aardvark’d: 12 Weeks with Geeks was co-produced and starred Joel in 2005. It was a documentary about the creation of remote support equipment known as Project Aardvark at Fog Creek.

    Joel and Jeff Atwood created Stack Overflow, a community network for software developers, three years later, in 2008. Joel led the business as CEO until October 1, 2019, when Prashanth Chandrasekhar took over. Since that time, Joel has led Stack Overflow as chairman.

    Joel founded Trello in 2011, an online project management tool that draws its inspiration from the Kanban approach. In 2016, Joel made public Anil Dash’s appointment as the new CEO of Fog Creek Software.
