    Meteorology

    Meteorology is a subfield of atmospheric sciences. It involves atmospheric physics and chemistry, as well as a concentration on weather forecasting, which aids in dealing with and preparing for potential severe weather. Although the study of the aforesaid topic extends back millennia, significant advancements in meteorology did not occur until the 18th century. Meteorological phenomena are described by variables in the Earth’s atmosphere, such as air pressure, water vapor, temperature, mass flow, and the interactions between the variables. Meteorology employs scales to forecast the weather at various levels. This field, along with climatology, atmospheric chemistry, and atmospheric physics, is a sub-discipline of atmospheric sciences.
