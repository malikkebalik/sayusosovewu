    Russell Westbrook is a basketball player who plays for Oklahoma City Thunder of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He was the “NBA Most Valuable Player” during the 2016-2017 season. When he scored a triple-double in a season, he became the second player to do so after Oscar Robertson. Representing his country, his team won a Gold medal in the 2012 London Olympics.

    Education

    Russell went to Leuzinger High School in Lawndale, California. At the time Russ was in high school, he went from standing 5’8 in his freshman year, to reaching his adult size of 6’3 before his senior year. With his huge statistics numbers, during his senior year, Russ made a name for himself and became a target of many college programs. However, after the offer of UCLA Bruins came on the desk, Westbrook accepted their full college scholarship.

    While being in UCLA, Westbrook made it to two consecutive Final Fours with his team, but both times, he came up short, losing in the semi-finals. After only 2 years of college, Russell entered the 2008 NBA draft in which he was selected as the 4th overall pick by the Seattle SuperSonics (Nowadays known as Oklahoma City Thunder).

    Brand Endorsements

    Westbrook has signed several sponsorship contracts since he entered the NBA. He signed with Jordan Brand in October 2012, and Kings and Jaxs Boxer Briefs in November 2013. In February 2015, Russell became a Marketing Creative Director of True Religion (a popular denim brand) and a global face of the drink Mountain Dew Kickstart produced by PepsiCo.

    Russell has also appeared in various commercial campaigns for brands such as Champs Sports, Subway and Levi Strauss & Co.

    Best Known For

    Westbrook is known for his inhuman athleticism. Many basketball experts explain his genetics as rarely seen even in a league as the NBA where there are so many well prepared and gifted athletes. He is mostly known for his hunger and passion for the game and also for his unseen efforts on the basketball court.

    First NBA Match

    Russ had his NBA debut on October 29, 2009, when his Oklahoma City Thunder faced Milwaukee Bucks. Westbrook came off the bench and played a total of 22 minutes. For the time spent on the court, Russell scored 13 points with 4 of 9 shooting backed up with 4 rebounds, 4 assists, and 1 block. However, OKC lost that game by 11, with the final result being 98-87.
